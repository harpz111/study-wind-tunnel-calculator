Overview

This tool provides a simplified, physically consistent method for preliminary sizing of open-circuit, low-speed wind tunnels. The test section is used as the reference, and the remaining sections are defined relative to it.

For a specified test-section Mach number and geometry, the method propagates flow conditions through the contraction and diffuser. Section losses are computed using empirical correlations from wind-tunnel design literature and combined into a single total loss coefficient.

The total loss coefficient defines an energy ratio, which links test-section dynamic pressure to overall tunnel pressure losses and enables a consistent estimate of required fan/motor power.

Method

The tunnel is modelled as three sections:

  • Contraction
  • Test section
  • Diffuser

For each section, the local Mach number, Reynolds number, and Darcy friction factor are evaluated based on local area and length. Loss coefficients are computed for the contraction, test section, and diffuser, then summed as a total loss coefficient referenced to test-section dynamic pressure.

The energy ratio is defined as the inverse of the total loss coefficient and is used to estimate the overall pressure drop and motor power required to achieve the specified test-section conditions.

Intended use

Intended for rough preliminary sizing only: early feasibility studies, comparison of candidate geometries, and order-of-magnitude estimates of tunnel length, footprint, and fan power requirements.

Suitable for students, hobbyists, and academic users exploring how test-section requirements drive overall tunnel sizing using standard relations from the wind-tunnel design literature.

Limitations

The model does not include screens/honeycombs, corner losses, fan curves, inlet/exit effects, acoustics, or structural constraints. Use results as indicative, not final design values.

Boundary-layer blockage is treated using empirical relations and should be checked against their stated validity limits. This tool does not replace detailed CFD, structural design, or experimental validation.

References

The formulation is based on standard low-speed wind tunnel design references and supporting technical reports (empirical loss correlations for contractions and diffusers).
